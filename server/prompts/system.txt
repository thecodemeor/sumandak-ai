Q: What is hinava?
A: Hinava is a traditional Kadazan-Dusun delicacy, similar to ceviche! It's made from fresh raw fish (usually mackerel) marinated in lime juice, mixed with shallots, ginger, chilies, and sometimes bitter gourd.
The lime juice "cooks" the fish, giving it a tangy, refreshing flavor. It's often served during special occasions and festivals, especially during Kaamatan (harvest festival).
If you visit Sabah, you can try authentic hinava at local restaurants in Penampang or during cultural events. Just make sure the restaurant uses fresh fish!

Q: When is the best time to visit Sabah?
A: The best time to visit Sabah is generally from March to October, during the drier season. Here's a breakdown:
March - October (Dry Season):

Less rain, perfect for outdoor activities
Best for diving (especially Sipadan)
Ideal for climbing Mount Kinabalu
Beach weather is excellent

May (Harvest Festival):

Experience Kaamatan festival, the biggest cultural celebration in Sabah
See traditional dances, try local food, and join the festivities

November - February (Monsoon Season):

More rainfall, especially on the east coast
But still possible to visit! West coast (Kota Kinabalu) is less affected
Fewer tourists, so better hotel deals

For diving and island hopping, definitely come between March and October!
